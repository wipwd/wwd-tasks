What is Tasks?

This project aims at providing a user-side, browser-based task tracker, following somewhat of a Kanban methodology.

What is Kanban?

Somewhere else you may find a far better explanation on what Kanban is, but in general terms it is a lean methodology to manage work. It may take many forms, but typically you'll have cards (in our case, tasks), which travel between different stages of a pipeline.

In our case, tasks will travel from a Backlog stage towards a Done stage. Tasks won't be able to travel back to previous stages, unless directly to the backlog for further assessment. Only tasks in the Done stage won't be able to move at all -- they are done, afterall.

What do you mean by Browser-based?

Essentially, nothing is kept on the server aside from the files required to run the application. The whole application in run in your browser, and all data is kept in the browser as well.

Where is my data kept?

In your browser. Ultimately this means your computer. In broad strokes, the data is kept in storage dedicated by your browser to this application, and is local and specific to that browser and that application: other browsers won't be able to access that information, nor will other applications or sites.

Can I see my tasks on my phone or other computer?

Sort of.

Given the local nature of this application, we do not share state with a server that is capable of synchronizing multiple clients in real time.

Instead, what we chose to do is allow you, the user, while being properly informed of how your data is going to be kept, to explicitly choose to synchronize your data with an external third-party storage service.

This synchronization is not real-time. You will have to do it each time you want to save your current state to a third-party remote site. This can be used as a backup, or to synchronize other devices by pulling your state from said third-party storage.

You can find the synchronization feature in Settings.
