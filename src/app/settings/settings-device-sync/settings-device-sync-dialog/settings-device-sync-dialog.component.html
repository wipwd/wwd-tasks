A WORD ON DEVICE SYNC

We synchronize your devices on a best effort basis. We will refuse to sync if we find changes that may conflict.

All data is encrypted in your browser before sending to the server, and decrypted upon receiving. Encryption relies on a user-provided password. Lose the password, lose the data.

The data is currently kept in Google's firebase. We'll have costs associated with usage. Thus we ask you to only sync when you have to, instead of synchronizing on every single change.

To synchronize your devices you will have to login using google. We need to ensure the storage is not abused by anonymous entities, so we decided this would be an acceptable required minimum. We don't keep, willingly, any information on you, but some data may be kept. For instance, all data is associated with your user id. But it's kept encrypted, so... there's that.

Please understand that once you use this feature, you will no longer have full authority over where your data is kept. We were promised somewhere in Europe, but we cannot guarantee more than the fact that the data is encrypted in your browser before being transfered.

We are also unable to guarantee that the encryption is foolproof, nor that it is unbreakable, now or in the future. We are using a reasonably well established library (keybase's triplesec), but you should not trust software completely, nor should you ever consider that any encryption mechanism will hold secure forever.

If you do not wish to risk your data, please hit Cancel. Otherwise we will assume you are generally okay with what we just explained, and understand that, from now on, your data will be utterly and completely out of your control.

This action will have to be performed on each sync.
